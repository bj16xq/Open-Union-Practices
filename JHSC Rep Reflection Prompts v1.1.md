# JHSC Representative Reflection Prompts

> “The unexamined life is not worth living”
> — Socrates, as cited by Plato, n.d., Apology (38a)

This resource is the meant to guide reflection on individual and group practices for JHSC Representatives. 

A useful prompt is start/stop/continue. [Video link](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=igg2LC6-iXY&pp=ygUTc3RhcnQvc3RvcC9jb250aW51ZQ%3D%3D). The process is to ask yourself:

What practices should I/we start doing that I/we haven't done yet?
What practices should I/we stop doing that isn’t working?
What practices should I/we continue doing that is working?

**I** = yourself as a JHSC representative  **We** = the JHSC as a whole
**Practices** = both formal processes as well as informal habits.

In addition from answering the **what**, you should also ask yourself **why** you should start/stop/continue that particular aspect of your practice to determine underlying interactions. Also, consider any potential latent effects/consequences, whether favourable or not.

For specificity, this reflection can be broken down into each of the various aspects of your role. For example, you should reflect separately for JHSC meetings and workplace inspections. See guiding questions in the table below.

| | |
|---|---|
| Meetings | Consider how well meetings went with respect to preparation, engage-ment both as an individual and as a collective group.<br>What has been the most successful JHSC meeting of the year? What made it successful? What should I continue doing to keep up this success?<br>What has been the least successful JHSC meeting of the year? What made it unsuccessful? What should I/we stop doing for meeting preparation or engagement?<br>Think about potential changes to meeting preparation or engagement.<br>What should I/we start doing for meeting preparation or engagement? Will there be any cons to doing so?|
| Inspections | Consider how well inspection went with respect to preparation, engagement, and reporting, both as an individual and as a collective group.<br>What has been the most successful JHSC inspection of the year? What made it successful? What should I continue doing to keep up this success?<br>What has been the least successful JHSC inspection of the year? What made it unsuccessful? What should I/we stop doing for inspection preparation, engagement, or reporting?<br>Think about potential changes to inspection preparation, engagement, or reporting.<br>What should I/we start doing for inspection preparation, engagement, or reporting? Will there be any cons to doing so? |
